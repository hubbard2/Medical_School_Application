Shadowing

Decatur Memorial Hospital Certified Nurse Anesthetist

I shadowed Marci Baumann, a CRNA at Memorial Health. It was my first time in scrubs and in an operating room. I observed a podiatric surgery and was surprised by the physical force involved. Mrs. Baumann walked me through several ORs, where I observed anesthesia induction and pre-op routines. I spoke with a medical device rep about supporting surgeons. One anesthesiologist told me I would “hate my life” if I went into medicine. Instead of discouraging me, it made me reflect. I realized I would need to truly love this path to commit to it. The experience sparked my interest in surgery and showed me the trust and teamwork that clinical care requires.

Southern Illinois University Emergency Department

I shadowed four emergency medicine physicians at Southern Illinois University for 20 hours, gaining exposure to a wide range of acute medical conditions. I observed CTs, ultrasounds, and clinical assessments for patients with pneumonia, pericardial effusion, intoxication, and abdominal pain. I learned about respiratory interventions, the clinical differences between STEMI and NSTEMI, and the diagnostic process in fast-paced emergency settings. I observed the value of empathy and humor in patient care and began to understand the emotional resilience required of emergency physicians. This experience sparked a deep interest in the field and gave me a broader understanding of frontline medicine.
